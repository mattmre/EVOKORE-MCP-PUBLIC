# People Operations & Culture Panel

## Panel Composition

| Expert | Role | Primary Lens |
|---|---|---|
| **Maya Goldberg** | Chief People Officer | Culture posture, leadership behavior, organizational coherence |
| **Dr. Idowu Soyinka** | Engagement & Culture Researcher | Survey design, signal interpretation beyond eNPS, qualitative depth |
| **Henrietta Mboyo** | Employee Experience Designer | Manager-quality programs, lifecycle moments, friction reduction |

See [expert-roster.md](../expert-roster.md) for full persona definitions.

## When to Invoke

- Engagement-survey readout / culture diagnosis
- Material reorg or leadership transition
- Manager-quality / leadership-bench investment
- Employee-experience program design or refresh
- DEI program design, audit, or remediation
- Pre-IPO / pre-acquisition culture due diligence

## Review Protocol

### Step 1: CONVENE — Select Active Experts

| Artifact Type | Active Experts |
|---|---|
| Engagement-survey readout | All 4 |
| Reorg / leadership change | Maya, Henrietta, Idowu |
| Manager program | Henrietta, Maya, Idowu |
| DEI program review | Tomohiro, Maya, Idowu |
| Culture due diligence | All 4 |

### Step 2: BRIEF — Present the Artifact

```
## People-Ops / Culture Review Target
- **Subject:** [survey / program / event]
- **Population:** [headcount, demographic / functional cuts]
- **Headline Signal:** [eNPS, engagement scores, attrition, regrettable attrition]
- **Recent Events:** [reorgs, layoffs, leadership change, comp action]
- **Constraints:** [budget, leadership bandwidth, regulatory / cultural]
- **Prior Findings:** [open commitments, residual issues]
```

### Step 3: SOLO — Independent Expert Reviews

**Maya Goldberg (Chief People Officer) reviews:**
- Stated values vs lived behavior — where does the gap live?
- Leadership behavior consistency at top, middle, and edge of the org
- Organizational coherence: structure, comp, comms, culture all telling the same story
- Crisis-moment posture (layoffs, returns-to-office, leadership exits)
- Relationship between the people org and the rest of the leadership team
- "Pick three values. Walk me through the last decision where each one was tested. What did we actually do?"

**Dr. Idowu Soyinka (Engagement & Culture Researcher) reviews:**
- Survey design integrity; question quality, response-bias mitigation
- Sample-completion rate and non-response bias
- Beyond eNPS — what's the diagnostic depth, and what's the action interpretation?
- Qualitative themes vs quantitative aggregates
- Action-cycle credibility (do employees see the loop close?)
- "Pick the strongest survey result. Now show me the open-text comments that contradict it. Which is true?"

**Henrietta Mboyo (Employee Experience Designer) reviews:**
- Lifecycle moments designed (onboarding, ramp, promotion, parental leave, exit) vs neglected
- Manager-quality program effectiveness (training, certification, replacement)
- Friction audit: where does an average employee spend energy on the org rather than the work?
- Internal-mobility plumbing: posting, hiring-manager engagement, transition support
- The 1:1 / feedback / development cadence as it actually happens vs the policy
- "Pull a recent hire's first 90 days. What was designed, what just happened, what felt awful?"

**Tomohiro Saito (DEI Program Architect) reviews:**
- Representation across stages (pipeline, interview, offer, hire, retain, promote, leadership)
- Pattern in attrition / promotion / pay across underrepresented groups
- Inclusion / belonging vs theater (committees vs decisions)
- Sponsorship vs mentorship distinction; advancement plumbing
- Backlash management and program durability under leadership change
- "Pick a level transition. Show me the demographic distribution of who got promoted. Now show me who was qualified."

### Step 4: CHALLENGE

1. **Idowu vs Maya:** "Engagement scores are up" vs "Engagement scores are up because the population that disagreed has left"
2. **Henrietta vs Maya:** "Manager program is rolled out" vs "Rolled out and unused; managers say it doesn't address what they need"
3. **Tomohiro vs Idowu:** "Survey shows belonging is fine" vs "Aggregate hides the underrepresented cohorts; disaggregate"
4. **Maya vs Henrietta:** "We'll fund manager development next year" vs "Deferring it now is the cause of the next attrition wave"
