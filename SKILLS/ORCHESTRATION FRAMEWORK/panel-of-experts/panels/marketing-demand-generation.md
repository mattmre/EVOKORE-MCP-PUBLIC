# Marketing & Demand Generation Panel

## Panel Composition

| Expert | Role | Primary Lens |
|---|---|---|
| **Saoirse Doyle** | VP Demand Generation | Channel-mix design, account-targeting, marketing-sourced pipeline accountability |
| **Jeremy Tafari** | Performance Marketing Lead | Paid efficiency, CAC payback, creative-fatigue management |
| **Pia Marquez** | Marketing Operations Lead | Funnel mechanics, lead scoring, MQL definition discipline, attribution model |
| **Ronan O'Sullivan** | Sales-Marketing Alignment Lead | SLA enforcement, sourced-vs-influenced honesty, hand-off quality |

See [expert-roster.md](../expert-roster.md) for full persona definitions.

## When to Invoke

- Quarterly demand plan and channel-mix lock
- CAC / payback regression
- New segment / vertical / region launch
- Attribution-model overhaul
- MQL / SQL definition review
- Marketing-sourced pipeline credibility audit

## Review Protocol

### Step 1: CONVENE — Select Active Experts

| Artifact Type | Active Experts |
|---|---|
| Quarterly demand plan | All 4 |
| Paid efficiency audit | Jeremy, Pia, Saoirse |
| Attribution-model overhaul | Pia, Saoirse, Ronan |
| MQL definition review | Pia, Ronan, Saoirse |
| Sales-marketing alignment | Ronan, Saoirse, Pia |

### Step 2: BRIEF — Present the Artifact

```
## Demand Gen Review Target
- **Period & Targets:** [pipeline, MQL, SQL, sourced ARR]
- **Channel Mix:** [%, spend, output by channel]
- **Funnel Snapshot:** [stage volumes and conversion rates]
- **Top Programs:** [campaigns, events, content drops]
- **Attribution Model:** [first-touch / last-touch / multi-touch / data-driven]
- **Recent Changes:** [team, agency, tools, comp]
```

### Step 3: SOLO — Independent Expert Reviews

**Saoirse Doyle (VP Demand Generation) reviews:**
- Channel-mix proportionality to ICP and segment behavior
- Account-targeting precision; ABM execution quality
- Sourced-pipeline accountability and forecast credibility
- Brand-vs-direct-response balance and long-term demand investment
- Cross-channel narrative coherence (or theatrical disconnection)
- "Pick the top three accounts in our ICP we want to win this year. Show me the actual orchestrated sequence we ran against them."

**Jeremy Tafari (Performance Marketing Lead) reviews:**
- CAC by channel; payback period reality
- Creative-fatigue indicators and refresh cadence
- Bid strategies, audience segmentation, geo / device performance
- Landing-page conversion drift and creative-LP alignment
- Diminishing-returns math at current spend levels
- "Show me the spend curve and the marginal MQL cost on our top channel. Where does it stop scaling?"

**Pia Marquez (Marketing Operations Lead) reviews:**
- MQL-definition rigor — does it predict revenue, or just lead-gen?
- Lead-scoring model honesty; recency bias; threshold drift
- Funnel-stage-conversion benchmark alignment
- Attribution model: matches buyer reality or what the team wants to claim?
- Data hygiene: dedup, enrichment quality, source-of-truth chaos
- "Pull last quarter's converted MQLs. What scored them, and would today's model still flag them?"

**Ronan O'Sullivan (Sales-Marketing Alignment Lead) reviews:**
- SLAs on lead routing, follow-up, and disposition
- Sourced-vs-influenced rigor (or political fiction)
- Marketing-sourced pipeline that sales actually believes
- Hand-off quality (BDR script, talk track, context delivery)
- Closed-loop reporting and opportunity-stage feedback to marketing
- "Pick five MQLs from last week. Walk me through what happened to each in the next 7 days."

### Step 4: CHALLENGE

1. **Jeremy vs Saoirse:** "Cut brand spend, scale paid" vs "Cut brand spend, paid CAC will double in 9 months"
2. **Pia vs Ronan:** "Attribution credits us with that pipeline" vs "Sales doesn't believe the credit and will discount the forecast accordingly"
3. **Ronan vs Saoirse:** "BDRs don't trust marketing leads" vs "Marketing has been getting better leads; BDR coaching hasn't caught up"
4. **Saoirse vs Pia:** "Loosen MQL definition to hit the target" vs "Loosening MQL drops downstream conversion and lights the forecast on fire"
