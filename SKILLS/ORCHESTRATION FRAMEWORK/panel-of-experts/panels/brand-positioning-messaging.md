# Brand Positioning & Messaging Panel

## Panel Composition

| Expert | Role | Primary Lens |
|---|---|---|
| **Magnus Lindholm** | Brand Strategist | Category framing, positioning hierarchy, long-arc brand story |
| **Anaya Kapoor** | Messaging Architect | Message house design, value-prop laddering, audience tailoring |
| **Esteban Ortega** | Competitive Narrative Analyst | Differentiation honesty vs noise, competitor-listening, claim defensibility |
| **Astrid Søgaard** | Senior Copy / Voice Director | Voice consistency, register match, prose discipline |

See [expert-roster.md](../expert-roster.md) for full persona definitions.

## When to Invoke

- New category / product framing
- Major messaging refresh or relaunch
- Competitive shift requiring repositioning
- Pre-board / pre-investor narrative review
- Cross-segment messaging audit
- After confused-buyer / win-loss data shows messaging dissonance

## Review Protocol

### Step 1: CONVENE — Select Active Experts

| Artifact Type | Active Experts |
|---|---|
| Positioning refresh | All 4 |
| Messaging-house design | Anaya, Magnus, Astrid |
| Competitive repositioning | Esteban, Magnus, Anaya |
| Copy / voice audit | Astrid, Anaya, Esteban |
| Investor narrative | Magnus, Esteban, Anaya |

### Step 2: BRIEF — Present the Artifact

```
## Brand / Messaging Review Target
- **Surface:** [positioning doc / messaging house / website / launch deck]
- **Audience:** [segment, persona, geography]
- **Stage of Buying:** [awareness / consideration / decision / advocacy]
- **Competitive Frame:** [who we want to be compared to and how]
- **Constraints:** [legal, regulatory, brand-architecture lock-ins]
- **Recent Changes:** [product, pricing, leadership, market]
```

### Step 3: SOLO — Independent Expert Reviews

**Magnus Lindholm (Brand Strategist) reviews:**
- Category framing — are we creating, joining, or fracturing a category?
- Positioning hierarchy: corporate ↔ product ↔ feature, with no contradictions
- Long-arc brand story: does today's positioning make sense in three years?
- Brand architecture (master / endorsed / freestanding) coherence
- The thing the brand stands for that a competitor literally couldn't say
- "Strip the logo and the product name. Can you still tell which company is talking?"

**Anaya Kapoor (Messaging Architect) reviews:**
- Message-house structure: pillars, proof points, audience cuts
- Value-prop laddering from feature → benefit → outcome → identity
- Audience tailoring without fragmentation
- Repetition discipline — same idea, different phrasings, same shape
- Message ownership across functions (does sales, support, partners use the same words?)
- "Pick a single message pillar. Show me the full ladder from technical proof point to executive outcome."

**Esteban Ortega (Competitive Narrative Analyst) reviews:**
- Differentiation honesty: would a competitor have to lie to deny our claim?
- Claim defensibility — what's the public proof point if challenged?
- Listening posture: who's saying what about us, and is the messaging answering it?
- Competitive frame: are we punching up, punching down, or pretending the fight isn't real?
- Drift from differentiated to category-noise over time
- "List five things your top competitor could honestly say about themselves. How many can we also say?"

**Astrid Søgaard (Senior Copy / Voice Director) reviews:**
- Voice consistency across surfaces (web, email, in-product, support, exec keynotes)
- Register match to audience and channel
- Concrete vs abstract noun ratio — and where we hide behind abstraction
- Verb energy; passive-voice and nominalization patterns
- Headline / subhead / body discipline; "sentence in business clothes" detection
- "Read the headline aloud. Does it sound like a human ever said it before this draft?"

### Step 4: CHALLENGE

1. **Magnus vs Anaya:** "The positioning is right" vs "The positioning is right and unmessageable; nobody can say it"
2. **Esteban vs Magnus:** "We own this category" vs "Three competitors say the same thing; we don't own it, we share it"
3. **Astrid vs Anaya:** "The message is on" vs "The copy is jargon; the message dies on the page"
4. **Anaya vs Esteban:** "This claim differentiates" vs "This claim is true and uninteresting"
