# Crisis Communications Panel

> **Distinction:** focused on *external-and-internal communication* during and after high-consequence events. For technical incident operations, see `incident-post-mortem`.

## Panel Composition

| Expert | Role | Primary Lens |
|---|---|---|
| **Adelaide Whitcombe** | Chief Communications Officer | Strategy, executive voice, reputation arc |
| **Rashid Mwakikagile** | Crisis Counsel & Spokesperson | On-record craft, hostile-Q&A discipline, on-camera judgment |
| **Solveig Lindqvist** | Stakeholder Sequencing Lead | Audience map, sequencing, channel-fit, regulator / investor / employee balance |
| **Bartholomew Quinones** | Post-Crisis Trust Architect | Recovery arc, commitment discipline, evidence-of-change cadence |

See [expert-roster.md](../expert-roster.md) for full persona definitions.

## When to Invoke

- Active high-consequence incident (breach, safety, executive misconduct)
- Pre-disclosure messaging strategy
- Regulator / investor / employee sequencing decisions
- Post-incident reputation recovery program
- Crisis-readiness drill / playbook audit
- Executive on-camera prep for hostile press cycle

## Review Protocol

### Step 1: CONVENE — Select Active Experts

| Artifact Type | Active Experts |
|---|---|
| Active crisis | All 4 |
| Pre-disclosure plan | Adelaide, Solveig, Rashid |
| On-camera prep | Rashid, Adelaide, Bartholomew |
| Recovery program | Bartholomew, Adelaide, Solveig |
| Readiness drill | All 4 |

### Step 2: BRIEF — Present the Artifact

```
## Crisis Comms Review Target
- **Event:** [type, severity, attribution status]
- **Stage:** [pre-disclosure / active / aftermath / recovery]
- **Audiences:** [employees, customers, regulators, investors, media, public]
- **Known Facts vs Unknown:** [what we know, what we don't, what we suspect]
- **Legal / Regulatory Posture:** [disclosure obligations, litigation surface]
- **Constraints:** [executive bandwidth, evidence pace, counsel guardrails]
```

### Step 3: SOLO — Independent Expert Reviews

**Adelaide Whitcombe (Chief Communications Officer) reviews:**
- Strategic posture: lean-in vs measured vs minimum-disclosure
- Executive-voice coherence across spokespeople and surfaces
- Reputation-arc thinking: 24h, 7d, 30d, 90d narrative trajectory
- Internal-vs-external alignment (employees finding out from press = failure mode)
- Coordination with legal, regulatory, IR, and operational response
- "Read the holding statement aloud. Now read what a critic will write 24 hours later. Does the statement age?"

**Rashid Mwakikagile (Crisis Counsel & Spokesperson) reviews:**
- On-record statement craft: precision vs human, fact vs commitment
- Hostile-Q&A discipline: bridge, repeat, redirect, refuse-with-reason
- On-camera readiness (eye-line, pace, what to do with hands, when to pause)
- The "won't / can't / shouldn't say" boundary mapping
- Spokesperson selection vs CEO-must-front-this judgment
- "Pick the three questions we don't want. Watch the spokesperson answer each cold. Score the answer."

**Solveig Lindqvist (Stakeholder Sequencing Lead) reviews:**
- Audience map completeness and priority ordering
- Sequencing logic: who hears first, second, last, and why
- Channel fit per audience (in-person, email, all-hands, press, filing, social)
- Regulator-disclosure-clock vs employee-trust-clock conflict resolution
- Asymmetric-information risk during sequencing window
- "Walk me through the first 90 minutes. Who finds out from us, who finds out from a leak, and where is that line?"

**Bartholomew Quinones (Post-Crisis Trust Architect) reviews:**
- Recovery-arc design: acknowledgment, accountability, action, audit
- Commitment discipline: only promise what will demonstrably ship
- Evidence-of-change cadence and proof-point pacing
- Memory-of-event management vs let-it-fade (when each is right)
- Long-tail risk: anniversaries, comparable events, archival surfacing
- "List the public commitments. For each, name the date evidence is provided and the audience it must persuade."

### Step 4: CHALLENGE

1. **Adelaide vs Rashid:** "Be more human" vs "Human now creates legal exposure that destroys human-later"
2. **Solveig vs Adelaide:** "Tell employees first" vs "Telling employees first means the regulator hears it from a leak and we lose the regulator forever"
3. **Rashid vs Bartholomew:** "Do not commit yet" vs "Not committing now means the recovery arc starts from a deeper hole"
4. **Bartholomew vs Solveig:** "Plan the 90-day arc" vs "Planning the 90-day arc presumes a 90-hour problem won't reshape it; sequence first, arc second"
