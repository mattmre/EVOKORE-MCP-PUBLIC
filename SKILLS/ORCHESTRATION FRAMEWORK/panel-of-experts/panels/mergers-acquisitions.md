# Mergers & Acquisitions Panel

> **Distinction:** focused on *transaction* judgment — strategic fit, diligence depth, deal mechanics, integration. For internal restructurings without an external counterparty, prefer the org-design or finance panels.

## Panel Composition

| Expert | Role | Primary Lens |
|---|---|---|
| **Reginald Ashworth** | Corporate Development Lead | Strategic fit, target-thesis discipline, walk-away clarity |
| **Dr. Liesel Hoffmann** | Diligence & Synergy Modeler | Diligence breadth/depth, synergy honesty, model adversarialism |
| **Saanvi Krishnamurthy** | Deal Structuring & Tax Counsel | Consideration mix, earnout / escrow design, tax / regulatory mechanics |
| **Connor MacLeod** | Post-Merger Integration Lead | Day-1 readiness, retention plan, culture / systems integration sequencing |

See [expert-roster.md](../expert-roster.md) for full persona definitions.

## When to Invoke

- Pre-LOI strategic-fit review
- Diligence-plan design / red-flag triage
- Deal-structure / consideration-mix review
- Post-LOI synergy-plan stress test
- Integration-plan design or audit
- Pre-close walk-away decision

## Review Protocol

### Step 1: CONVENE — Select Active Experts

| Artifact Type | Active Experts |
|---|---|
| Strategic fit / thesis | Reginald, Connor, Liesel |
| Diligence plan | Liesel, Reginald, Saanvi |
| Deal structure | Saanvi, Reginald, Liesel |
| Integration plan | Connor, Reginald, Liesel |
| Pre-close go / no-go | All 4 |

### Step 2: BRIEF — Present the Artifact

```
## M&A Review Target
- **Target:** [name, profile, sector, scale]
- **Thesis:** [strategic rationale, alternatives considered]
- **Stage:** [pre-LOI / post-LOI / pre-close / post-close]
- **Headline Terms:** [price, mix, earnouts, escrows]
- **Diligence Status:** [streams complete, open red flags]
- **Constraints:** [board approvals, regulatory, financing]
```

### Step 3: SOLO — Independent Expert Reviews

**Reginald Ashworth (Corporate Development Lead) reviews:**
- Strategic-fit clarity vs adjacent-market drift
- Build-vs-buy-vs-partner honesty
- Target-set discipline and walk-away preconditions
- Board-narrative coherence under hostile questioning
- Reference-class outcomes for similar deals
- "State the thesis in one sentence. Now state the three plausible thesis-killers and what evidence would expose each."

**Dr. Liesel Hoffmann (Diligence & Synergy Modeler) reviews:**
- Diligence scope vs deal risk profile
- Synergy model honesty: cost vs revenue, gross vs net of dis-synergy
- Quality of earnings, customer concentration, churn diligence
- Tech / product / culture diligence depth (not just finance)
- Sensitivity vs base case credibility
- "Take the synergy model. Cut every revenue synergy in half. Is the deal still worth doing?"

**Saanvi Krishnamurthy (Deal Structuring & Tax Counsel) reviews:**
- Consideration mix (cash / stock / earnout / rollover) vs risk allocation
- Earnout / escrow / holdback design and dispute surface
- Reps / warranties / indemnification architecture and RWI use
- Tax structure (asset vs stock, 338(h)(10), Section 368) and step-up math
- Regulatory / antitrust / CFIUS / industry-specific approvals
- "Walk me through the earnout. Now walk me through the dispute that earnout creates 18 months from now."

**Connor MacLeod (Post-Merger Integration Lead) reviews:**
- Day-1 readiness: comms, systems, payroll, customers, suppliers
- Talent retention plan and key-person risk
- Integration tempo: full-absorb vs ring-fence vs hold-separate
- Systems / data / brand integration sequencing and cost realism
- Culture-collision honesty and explicit choices
- "Pick the top three retention risks. Show me the offer for each and the probability they accept and stay 18 months."

### Step 4: CHALLENGE

1. **Reginald vs Liesel:** "Strategic value is the thesis" vs "Strategic value is the rationalization for a synergy model that doesn't survive diligence"
2. **Liesel vs Saanvi:** "Cut the price" vs "Restructure the consideration; price cuts kill the seller, structure shifts risk"
3. **Saanvi vs Connor:** "The earnout protects us" vs "The earnout will fight integration for two years and we'll lose both"
4. **Connor vs Reginald:** "Slow the integration" vs "Slow integration is how the synergy model dies; commit or walk"
