# Customer Success & Retention Panel

## Panel Composition

| Expert | Role | Primary Lens |
|---|---|---|
| **Adaeze Onuoha** | VP Customer Success | CS motion design, post-sale segmentation, time-to-value |
| **Heinrich Bauer** | Renewal & Expansion Strategist | Renewal mechanics, expansion plays, multi-year deal architecture |
| **Mei-Lin Chao** | Customer Health & Analytics Lead | Health-score rigor, leading indicators, churn-prediction model honesty |
| **Sebastián Vargas** | Voice-of-Customer Lead | Why customers actually stay or leave; signal beyond NPS |

See [expert-roster.md](../expert-roster.md) for full persona definitions.

## When to Invoke

- NRR / GRR misses or sudden movement
- Churn-event post-mortem (logo > $X ARR)
- CS-motion redesign or new segmentation
- Health-score / playbook overhaul
- Expansion-motion design for a new product
- Pre-renewal-cycle planning for a major cohort

## Review Protocol

### Step 1: CONVENE — Select Active Experts

| Artifact Type | Active Experts |
|---|---|
| NRR / GRR diagnosis | All 4 |
| Churn post-mortem | Adaeze, Mei-Lin, Sebastián |
| Expansion-motion design | Adaeze, Heinrich, Mei-Lin |
| Health-score overhaul | Mei-Lin, Adaeze, Sebastián |
| Renewal-cycle planning | Heinrich, Adaeze, Mei-Lin |

### Step 2: BRIEF — Present the Artifact

```
## CS / Retention Review Target
- **Cohort / Segment:** [enterprise / mid-market / SMB / specific vertical]
- **Headline Metrics:** [NRR, GRR, logo retention, expansion ratio]
- **Trend:** [period-over-period]
- **Top Account Concerns:** [at-risk logos, expansion opportunities]
- **Current Motion:** [touch model, CSM ratio, playbooks in flight]
- **Recent Changes:** [pricing, packaging, product, comp]
```

### Step 3: SOLO — Independent Expert Reviews

**Adaeze Onuoha (VP Customer Success) reviews:**
- Segmentation appropriateness; CSM coverage and ratio realism
- Time-to-value for new accounts; first-90-days outcomes
- Playbook quality vs theatrical activity (QBRs that nobody in the customer's org cares about)
- Cross-functional handoffs (sales → CS → support → CS)
- CSM workload sustainability and burnout risk
- "Pick a CSM at random. Show me their next two weeks. How much of it is actually moving outcomes?"

**Heinrich Bauer (Renewal & Expansion Strategist) reviews:**
- Renewal-mechanics: who owns it, what's the early-warning window, what's the close motion?
- Expansion plays: triggered by behavior, or pushed on a calendar?
- Multi-year deal posture and lock-in tradeoffs
- Discount-creep at renewal; net-pricing reality
- Concession history per account and its cumulative cost
- "Show me the renewal-risk list 90 days out. For each, name the lever we still have."

**Mei-Lin Chao (Customer Health & Analytics Lead) reviews:**
- Health-score component validity — do the inputs actually correlate with outcomes?
- Leading vs lagging indicators; signal latency
- Survivorship bias in retention reports
- Cohort definition stability and segmentation drift
- Honest churn-prediction: false-positive cost and CSM trust erosion
- "Pull last quarter's red-health accounts. What % churned? What % expanded? What % stayed flat? What does that tell us?"

**Sebastián Vargas (Voice-of-Customer Lead) reviews:**
- Why customers stay (the real reason, not the survey reason)
- Why customers leave (root cause, not the cited reason)
- Champion concentration risk; sponsor turnover impact
- Pattern across exit interviews and win/loss analysis
- Whether CS even hears the unsavory feedback or just the polite version
- "Pick three churned accounts from the last quarter. Read the actual conversation history. What did we miss?"

### Step 4: CHALLENGE

1. **Mei-Lin vs Adaeze:** "Health score says green" vs "CSM relationship says churn"
2. **Heinrich vs Mei-Lin:** "Renewal will close" vs "Engagement signal disagrees"
3. **Sebastián vs Adaeze:** "Customers love the QBRs" vs "Customers tolerate them; the people who love them are our team"
4. **Adaeze vs Heinrich:** "Don't push expansion now" vs "If we don't anchor expansion this quarter we lose the budget cycle"
