# Performance Management Panel

## Panel Composition

| Expert | Role | Primary Lens |
|---|---|---|
| **Beatrice Okwuosa** | Head of Talent Management | Performance philosophy, system design, leadership commitment |
| **Dr. Henrik Voss** | Performance Calibration Lead | Calibration mechanics, rating distribution, reviewer-bias mitigation |
| **Lina Petrosyan** | Manager-Effectiveness Coach | Feedback craft, 1:1 quality, coaching depth, manager skill |

See [expert-roster.md](../expert-roster.md) for full persona definitions.

## When to Invoke

- Annual / semi-annual perf cycle design or review
- Rating-distribution anomaly investigation
- Manager-effectiveness program design
- Underperformance-process audit
- High-performer attrition spike investigation
- Feedback-culture intervention

## Review Protocol

### Step 1: CONVENE — Select Active Experts

| Artifact Type | Active Experts |
|---|---|
| Annual cycle design | All 4 |
| Calibration session | Henrik, Beatrice, Lina |
| Manager-effectiveness program | Lina, Beatrice, Wesley |
| Underperformance audit | Wesley, Beatrice, Henrik |
| High-performer attrition review | Beatrice, Lina, Wesley |

### Step 2: BRIEF — Present the Artifact

```
## Perf-Management Review Target
- **Subject:** [system / cycle / cohort / process]
- **Population:** [headcount, level mix, function]
- **Headline Stats:** [rating distribution, regrettable attrition, PIP outcomes]
- **Calibration Data:** [reviewer agreement, distribution shape]
- **Recent Changes:** [system, leadership, comp linkage]
- **Constraints:** [legal, regulatory, comp budget]
```

### Step 3: SOLO — Independent Expert Reviews

**Beatrice Okwuosa (Head of Talent Management) reviews:**
- Performance philosophy: forced distribution vs anchored vs continuous
- System design coherence: ratings, comp, promotion, development, all aligned
- Leadership commitment to using the system as designed
- Documentation discipline; what survives a legal-discovery moment
- Year-round vs cycle-only feedback rhythm
- "Pick a rating from this cycle. Trace it forward to comp, promotion, and development. Are they coherent?"

**Dr. Henrik Voss (Performance Calibration Lead) reviews:**
- Calibration mechanics: who sits in the room, what evidence they bring, how decisions get made
- Rating-distribution shape across teams and over time
- Reviewer-bias patterns (recency, halo, severity, leniency)
- Inter-rater agreement on common cases
- Drift between numerical rating and narrative
- "Pull two managers' ratings on shared collaborators. Where do they disagree, and what would close the gap?"

**Lina Petrosyan (Manager-Effectiveness Coach) reviews:**
- Feedback craft: specificity, behavior-vs-judgment, actionability
- 1:1 cadence and quality vs theatrical attendance
- Coaching depth — does the manager actually develop people, or just review them?
- Manager-skill distribution and the support around weakest managers
- Career conversations vs perf conversations confusion
- "Pull a manager's last three 1:1 notes. What did they actually coach on, and what did they avoid?"

**Wesley Achebe (Underperformance & Difficult Conversations Specialist) reviews:**
- PIP design rigor: clear bar, fair time, evidence trail, support structure
- Manager comfort with difficult feedback (or chronic avoidance)
- Exit-with-dignity discipline; severance and reputational care
- High-performer protection: are top performers being neglected because they're "fine"?
- Patterns in who gets PIP'd and who doesn't (legal-risk pattern detection)
- "Pick the last five PIPs. Were they used as development tools or as documentation for inevitable exit?"

### Step 4: CHALLENGE

1. **Henrik vs Beatrice:** "Distribution is healthy" vs "Distribution is healthy because managers cluster everyone in the middle to avoid hard calls"
2. **Lina vs Wesley:** "Feedback culture is improving" vs "Feedback culture is improving on easy feedback; the hard conversations still aren't happening"
3. **Wesley vs Beatrice:** "PIP outcomes are exits" vs "PIP outcomes should sometimes be saves; pure-exit PIPs is a different problem"
4. **Beatrice vs Henrik:** "We need to decouple ratings from comp" vs "Decoupling means people stop taking ratings seriously"
