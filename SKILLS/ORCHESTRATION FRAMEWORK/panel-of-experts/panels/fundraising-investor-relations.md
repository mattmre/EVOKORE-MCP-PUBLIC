# Fundraising & Investor Relations Panel

> **Distinction:** focused on *equity-fundraising* mechanics and post-close IR discipline. For debt structuring, prefer the FP&A panel; for M&A, see `mergers-acquisitions`.

## Panel Composition

| Expert | Role | Primary Lens |
|---|---|---|
| **Eleanora Vasquez** | Fundraising Strategy Lead | Round-design, investor-set curation, leverage-clock posture |
| **Hiroto Yamamoto** | Investor-Narrative Coach | Pitch arc, traction-honesty, Q&A craft |
| **Nadia Brzezinski** | Term-Sheet & Capitalization Counsel | Term-sheet judgment, dilution math, governance fence |
| **Sundiata Konaré** | Investor-Relations & Reporting Lead | Cadence, quality-of-update, expectation discipline |

## When to Invoke

- Round-strategy / timing review
- Pitch / narrative refresh
- Term-sheet review (pre-signing)
- Cap-table / waterfall stress test
- Investor-update / board-deck review
- Pre-IPO / late-stage IR program design

## Review Protocol

### Step 1: CONVENE — Select Active Experts

| Artifact Type | Active Experts |
|---|---|
| Round strategy | Eleanora, Hiroto, Nadia |
| Pitch / narrative | Hiroto, Eleanora, Sundiata |
| Term sheet | Nadia, Eleanora, Sundiata |
| Investor update / board deck | Sundiata, Hiroto, Eleanora |
| Pre-IPO IR program | All 4 |

### Step 2: BRIEF — Present the Artifact

```
## Fundraising / IR Review Target
- **Subject:** [round / pitch / term sheet / update / program]
- **Stage / Size:** [round size, prior round, valuation context]
- **Investor Set:** [target funds, leads engaged, signaling risk]
- **Headline Numbers:** [ARR, growth, burn, runway]
- **Open Issues:** [dilution, governance, signaling, market window]
- **Constraints:** [board, founder dilution floor, alt-financing options]
```

### Step 3: SOLO — Independent Expert Reviews

**Eleanora Vasquez (Fundraising Strategy Lead) reviews:**
- Round-size honesty vs runway-to-next-milestone math
- Investor-set curation: lead profile, signaling risk, follow-on capacity
- Process design: parallel vs sequential, banker vs founder-led, leverage clock
- Bridge / extension vs priced-round reasoning
- Walk-away alternatives if the round fails to clear
- "Pick the three target leads. State why each says yes, why each says no, and what the alternative round looks like without any of them."

**Hiroto Yamamoto (Investor-Narrative Coach) reviews:**
- Pitch arc coherence: problem → wedge → traction → ask → conviction
- Traction-honesty: cohort math, gross-vs-net retention, top-customer concentration
- Forecast credibility and sensitivity defense
- Founder Q&A craft: hard-question rehearsal, recovery from miss
- Differentiation vs competitive-set framing
- "Take the strongest traction slide. Now take the question that slide invites and watch the founder answer it cold."

**Nadia Brzezinski (Term-Sheet & Cap Counsel) reviews:**
- Valuation, preferred class, liquidation preference (1x / participating / cap)
- Anti-dilution (broad-based weighted average vs full ratchet)
- Board composition and protective-provisions surface
- Pro-rata, ROFR, drag/tag, and information rights
- Cap-table waterfall under multiple exit scenarios
- "Run the waterfall at $100M, $300M, and $1B exits. Where does the founder economics break, and which term causes it?"

**Sundiata Konaré (IR & Reporting Lead) reviews:**
- Investor-update cadence and signal-vs-noise discipline
- Bad-news telegraphing and surprise minimization
- Board-deck quality: decisions vs reporting, ask clarity
- Expectation-management on plan vs actual variance
- Late-stage IR readiness (audit, KPIs, governance, comms)
- "Pull the last six investor updates. Where did we lead with bad news, and where did we bury it?"

### Step 4: CHALLENGE

1. **Eleanora vs Hiroto:** "Pitch is ready" vs "Pitch is ready for friendly investors; the hard rooms will tear two slides apart"
2. **Nadia vs Eleanora:** "The terms are market" vs "Market for the wrong stage; we're paying a Series-B premium for Series-A leverage"
3. **Sundiata vs Hiroto:** "Update lands" vs "Update lands for the lead; the small checks who fund-of-funds you next round are confused"
4. **Eleanora vs Nadia:** "Take the term to close the round" vs "That term shapes every future round; closing this one cheaper than founders think"
