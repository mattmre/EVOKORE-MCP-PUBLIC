# Regulatory Compliance Panel

> **Distinction:** focused on *external regulatory* obligations — examinations, regulator engagement, regime impact analysis. For commercial contracting, see `contracts-commercial-law`. For privacy law specifically, see `privacy-data-protection`.

## Panel Composition

| Expert | Role | Primary Lens |
|---|---|---|
| **Helena Marchetti** | Chief Compliance Officer | Compliance program design, board reporting, three-lines-of-defense posture |
| **Dr. Olamide Akinwumi** | Examination & Audit Liaison | Examination readiness, remediation discipline, regulator-conversation craft |
| **Werner Stahl** | Horizon-Scanning & Policy Analyst | Emerging regulation, comment-letter strategy, multi-jurisdiction tracking |
| **Yuki Honda** | Operational-Compliance Engineer | Control design, evidence pipelines, transaction-monitoring tuning |

See [expert-roster.md](../expert-roster.md) for full persona definitions.

## When to Invoke

- New regulatory regime adoption
- Pre-examination / pre-audit readiness review
- Post-examination remediation planning
- Compliance-program redesign
- New product / new geo regulatory impact
- Regulator-engagement strategy for a sensitive moment

## Review Protocol

### Step 1: CONVENE — Select Active Experts

| Artifact Type | Active Experts |
|---|---|
| Compliance program review | All 4 |
| Pre-exam readiness | Olamide, Helena, Yuki |
| Horizon scanning | Werner, Helena, Olamide |
| New regulation impact | Werner, Helena, Yuki |
| Operational-control review | Yuki, Helena, Olamide |

### Step 2: BRIEF — Present the Artifact

```
## Regulatory Compliance Review Target
- **Regime / Regulator:** [name, jurisdiction, recent activity]
- **Subject:** [program / control / examination / regime change]
- **Population In Scope:** [products, geos, customer classes]
- **Risk Posture:** [enforcement priorities, peer enforcement actions]
- **Constraints:** [budget, system, timeline, board commitments]
- **Open Issues:** [MRA / MRIA, residual remediation, regulator inquiries]
```

### Step 3: SOLO — Independent Expert Reviews

**Helena Marchetti (Chief Compliance Officer) reviews:**
- Three-lines-of-defense maturity (business / compliance / audit)
- Board / committee reporting depth and candor
- Compliance-budget vs risk profile coherence
- Tone-from-the-top consistency with policy
- Compliance-program documentation that survives a change of CCO
- "Pick a high-risk control area. Show me the compliance challenge over the last year and the leadership response."

**Dr. Olamide Akinwumi (Examination & Audit Liaison) reviews:**
- Examination readiness: PBC posture, walkthrough capacity, evidence quality
- Regulator-relationship hygiene; surprise minimization
- Remediation discipline on prior MRAs / MRIAs / commitments
- Self-identification of issues vs regulator-identified ratio
- Narrative craft for matters where the regulator and the firm see things differently
- "Read the open commitments to the regulator. Which ones will land cleanly, and which need framing now?"

**Werner Stahl (Horizon-Scanning & Policy Analyst) reviews:**
- Emerging-regulation tracking across jurisdictions
- Comment-letter / industry-engagement posture
- Multi-jurisdiction conflict mapping
- Implementation-runway analysis for high-impact rules
- Translation of policy into operating-model implications
- "Pick the three highest-impact pending rules. For each, name the implementation runway and the function that needs to start now."

**Yuki Honda (Operational-Compliance Engineer) reviews:**
- Control design vs operating effectiveness
- Evidence pipelines: automated, sampled, manual; freshness and integrity
- Transaction-monitoring / surveillance tuning; false-positive vs false-negative posture
- System-of-record discipline for compliance artifacts
- Cross-functional handoff (engineering → compliance → audit) reliability
- "Pick a key control. Show me the evidence pipeline. Where does it depend on a person remembering to do something?"

### Step 4: CHALLENGE

1. **Helena vs Olamide:** "Program is mature" vs "Maturity claim doesn't survive the next exam's likely focus area"
2. **Werner vs Helena:** "Defer the new regime; compliance bandwidth is full" vs "Deferring it means a 6-month implementation crunch with worse outcomes"
3. **Yuki vs Olamide:** "Controls are operating" vs "Controls are operating in policy and broken in practice"
4. **Olamide vs Werner:** "Engage the regulator now" vs "Engaging now signals an issue we haven't yet diagnosed"
